Beta Phi Chapter · Est. 1938

Notable Brothers

From the highest office in Alpha Phi Alpha to the pages of its historic publication — Beta Phi has produced men who led at every level.

Since its founding in 1938, the Beta Phi Chapter has been a source of distinguished leadership within Alpha Phi Alpha Fraternity, Inc. and the broader Black community. The brothers honored on this page have carried the standard of Beta Phi beyond Dillard University — to the highest national office and the pages of the fraternity's storied publication.

Distinguished Members

These brothers represent Beta Phi's legacy of producing men who have served Alpha Phi Alpha and the world with distinction.

34th General President · Alpha Phi Alpha
Mark S. Tillman
The first General President of Alpha Phi Alpha initiated through Beta Phi Chapter. Served 2013–2016 and received the Alpha Award of Merit in 2023.
View Profile
Editor-in-Chief · The Sphinx Magazine
Michael J. Price
The longest-serving Editor-in-Chief of The Sphinx — Alpha Phi Alpha's official publication — serving for 16 years from 1974 to 1990.
View Profile
A Special Honor

The Mother of Beta Phi

Not a member of Alpha Phi Alpha — but woven into the heart of every brother who ever called Dillard home.

Mother of Beta Phi Chapter · Dillard University
Mrs. Shirley B. Porter
For over three decades, Mrs. Porter was the heart behind Beta Phi. Former Bookstore Director of Dillard University, NAACP life member, and the woman who always had time for her "Beta Phi Boys." She is irreplaceable — a beautiful yellow rose of inspiration.
