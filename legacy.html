Bro. Mark S. Tillman

34th General President · Alpha Phi Alpha Fraternity, Inc.
Beta Phi Chapter Dillard University General President 2013–2016 Alpha Award of Merit — 2023

Bro. Mark S. Tillman is the 34th General President of Alpha Phi Alpha Fraternity, Inc., having served in that role from 2013 to 2016. He holds the historic distinction of being the first brother initiated through Beta Phi Chapter at Dillard University to ascend to the fraternity's highest office — a milestone that stands as one of the proudest chapters in Beta Phi's legacy.

Bro. Tillman was initiated at Dillard University and later became an active member of Gamma Lambda Chapter in Detroit, Michigan, where he served for more than 20 years as a tireless contributor to chapter programs and service projects. His election as General President also marked the first time in more than 30 years that a brother residing in the Midwestern Region held the office.

During his tenure, Bro. Tillman championed Alpha Phi Alpha's national initiatives in education, mentorship, and community service. He was a visible voice at the 50th Anniversary of the March on Washington, speaking at the Let Freedom Ring Celebration alongside Presidents Barack Obama, Bill Clinton, and Jimmy Carter — representing the fraternity on one of the most significant civic stages in recent memory.

In recognition of his decades of service and dedication to the aims of the fraternity, Bro. Tillman was awarded the Alpha Award of Merit at the 97th General Convention in Dallas, Texas in 2023 — by tradition and practice, the greatest honor bestowed upon a member of Alpha Phi Alpha.

Bro. Michael J. Price

Editor-in-Chief, The Sphinx · Beta Phi Chapter · In Memoriam
Beta Phi Chapter · Dillard University Editor-in-Chief, The Sphinx 1974–1990 In Memoriam · 1950–1990

Bro. Michael Jerome Price was born on November 28, 1950 in Yazoo City, Mississippi and grew up in Indianola, Mississippi. He received his B.A. and M.A. degrees in Philosophy from Dillard University in New Orleans in 1971 and from Purdue University in West Lafayette, Indiana in 1973 respectively. He entered Omega Chapter on July 23, 1990.

If ever a brother personified the Alpha Phi Alpha theme of Leadership and Service, it was dramatized in the life of Bro. Price. At Dillard, he served as Director of Communications and Programs — the culminating position in his service on the General Office staff that began in 1973. In 1974, he became Editor-in-Chief of The Sphinx, the nation's second oldest continuous African American journal, a role he held with distinction for sixteen years. As Alpha Phi Alpha's membership and structure grew, Bro. Price assumed responsibility for the fraternity's entire communications program and, in 1986, took on additional responsibility for providing oversight and support to the fraternity's national community programs.

In his staff capacity, Bro. Price was also a tireless leader in charting the future course of his beloved fraternity. He provided staff support to college and alumni chapters alike, served as liaison to committees, commissions, and foundations, and supported both elected and appointed fraternal officials — including five General Presidents. His thoughtful recommendations and direction assisted in the implementation of innovative internal policies and procedures as well as nationally recognized community service programs.

His legacy of leadership extended beyond the fraternity. He was a three-term President of Iota Delta Lambda Chapter and held active membership on the Editorial Board of the "Woodlawn Vision," the Black Independent Political Organization, and St. James United Methodist Church. He is listed in Who's Who Among Black Americans and Outstanding Young Men of America. He received the March of Dimes Birth Defects Foundation's Outstanding Service Award in 1986 and the fraternity's Distinguished Service Award in 1980, 1984, and 1988.

A national fraternity leader within the Black Greek-letter movement, Bro. Price faithfully accepted the arduous task of continuing the fraternal movement initiated by our beloved Jewels. The void left by his passing is immeasurable. His legacy stands as a reminder that all we do as Alpha men must be done in the noblest traditions of Alpha Phi Alpha.
